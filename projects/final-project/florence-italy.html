Location & Weather

Florence is located inland in the Tuscany region of Italy. Fun fact: in Italy, Florence is more often called Firenze!

Florence is surrounded by hills and mountains, and the Arno River runs through the city, creating beautiful panoramic views.
I visited Florence in the spring of 2022, and I believe that April is definitely the best month to go. The weather was absolutely perfect, with temperatures in the 70s (Fahrenheit) and clear skies every day!

Packing list

I recommend packing these things if you are visiting Florence, but always check the weather beforehand and pack accordingly!

  • Several T-shirts
  • A few pairs of cropped pants
  • A light jacket and/or sweater (for cooler nights)
  • A collared shirt or a blouse (for museums and restaurants)
  • A nice pair of slacks or a dress (see above)
  • Comfortable shoes (you will be doing a LOT of walking)
  • Sunglasses and/or a hat
  • Sunscreen!
  • A raincoat and umbrella (it didn't rain when I was there, but it's always a possibility!)
  • A small but secure bag for your belongings (I recommend a PacSafe crossbody or sling)

IMPORTANT: Don't forget your passport and other essential documents!

Landmarks & Attractions

Florence has a rich history and is home to dozens of museums and galleries. Even if you stay for several days, you won't be able to do and see everything. Fortunately, I've prepared a short list of some of Florence's more famous landmarks for you to visit!

Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore

The Florence Cathedral, also known as the Duomo, is famous for its iconic red dome, engineered by Filippo Brunelleschi in the 1400s.
The cathedral is now a museum, and houses many frescos and works of art. Guests are also able to appreciate the engineering and design of the cathedral itself.
Check the cathedral's website for museum hours and ticket availability.

The Uffizi Gallery

The Uffizi is home to dozens of famous paintings by artists such as Caravaggio, Botticelli, and Michelangelo, as well as other European artists.
The gallery also contains sculptures from the Medici family collection, as well as Roman copies of ancient Greek statues.
I must warn you: the Uffizi is big. It's definitely something you'll want to dedicate an entire day to.
Go to the official Uffizi Gallery website to check ticket prices and choose an entry timeslot.

La Galleria Dell'Accademia di Firenze

The Accademia Gallery in Florence holds paintings from the era of the Italian Renaissance as well as many sculptures, the most famous being Michelangelo's David.
I unfortunately did not have the chance to see it when I went to Florence, so I can only imagine how amazing it must look up close.
The Accademia's website contains information about its various exhibits, as well as how to purchase tickets.

There are so many other places to visit in Florence (such as the Ponte Vecchio, a famous bridge that crosses the river.) The ones I have listed here barely scratch the surface of things to do!
I recommend doing more research and planning out your trip as much as you can, to make the most of your time in Florence!

Food and Hotels

Florence has amazing restaurants and quality hotels that will elevate your trip! For hotels, I recommend the Hotel Spadai. It is centrally located and just a short walk from some of the attractions I listed above!
If you're looking for something a bit more scenic, the Gran Mediterrano offers beautiful views of the Arno, while still being relatively close to the center of Florence.

Florence, like many other places in Italy, has top-notch cuisine. As there are dozens of places to choose from, all with diverse food options, I recommend searching TripAdvisor (a list of Florentine restaurants is linked) or another website to find the place that sounds the best to you.
